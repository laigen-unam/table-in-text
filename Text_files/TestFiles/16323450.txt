Extended-spectrum beta-lactamase ( ESBL ) producing Enterobacteriaceae have become a worldwide problem 1 . 
In Bulgaria the first ESBL producers were described in 1992 2 . 
In Salmonella ESBL-pro-ducing strains have been observed in sporadic infections and occasionally in nosocomial outbreaks in various countries 3-10 . 
In this letter we characterize an isolate from a patient of a Salmonella serotype mainly known as food poisoning , Salmonella enterica Serotype Corvallis SZ 5-1 11 . 
It was cultured from the stool of a 45-day old baby suffering from gastroenteritis in September 2002 at the Thracian University Hospital , Stara Zagora , Bulgaria . 
The strain was identified as Salmonella by Crystal E/NF system ( Becton and Dickinson ) and serotyped as Salmonella Group C3 08 . 
Serotyping was confirmed at the National Reference Salmonella Laboratory . 
The antimicrobial susceptibilities were determined according to the NCCLS guidelines 12 . 
The wild type Salmonella strain was resistant to amoxicillin and piperacillin ( data not shown ) , tobramycin , gentamicin and chloramphenicol and cefotaxime .

The Salmonella strain expressed β-lactamases with pIs at 5.4 , 7.6 and 9.0 . 
Transconjugants produced only the enzymes with pI at 5.4 ( probable TEM-1 enzyme ) and pI 7.6 . 
Cefotaxime hydrolysis was restricted to the β-lactamase at pI 7.6 .

Using SHV-type specific primers , PCR products of the expected size of about 300 bp were obtained for wildtype and transconjugant strains . 
Sequencing of the entire coding region of blaSHV of the wild type strain and comparative analysis with published sequences ( accession no . 
X53433 ) revealed the presence of blaSHV-2 . 
This is the first report on the gastrointestinal occurrence of SHV-2-producing S. enterica Serotype Corvallis in Bulgaria . 
Its transmissible multiresistance including expanded spectum cephalosporins in combination with genes for resistance to chloramphenicol , tobramycin and gentamicin limits the therapeutic options and raises the risk for increasing ESBL multiresistant S. enterica and other Enterobacteriaceae in humans .
